02

The Ultimate Fracture

My hands still move, performing tasks they learned by rote,
Tidying spaces where your presence used to dwell;
But every action is a movement of a severed throat,
A soundless, choking spasm that I cannot tell.

I glance at walls that witnessed our old dreams,
Each picture frame now split by silent seams.
The air itself seems heavy with despair,
Memories cling to corners, everywhere.

The floor remembers footsteps I no longer make,
Every sound echoes a fragment I cannot shake.
I speak, but only whispers follow me,
A language broken by impossibility.

Yet still I move, though hollow and restrained,
A body functioning, yet my soul remained
In scattered pieces, floating, torn apart,
An endless fracture running through my heart.

03

The Reckoning of Annihilation

Now only a faint shadow stands within this place,
Where my true heart once lived, complete and whole;
In that shattered mirror is only the stain of a scorched candle’s grace,
Showing how even light itself surrenders its own soul.

I count my breaths one by one, a debt I must repay,
Because every new dawn is merely another installment of my decay.
Silence spreads its wings across the hollow room,
Dust settles where our laughter once bloomed.

The walls remember secrets I can no longer tell,
Echoes of a time before the final knell.
Each step forward is a surrender to despair,
Each heartbeat a reminder that I am bare.

Yet still I linger, in the ruins of what was,
Searching for fragments, for some hidden cause.
The reckoning comes slowly, relentless and cold,
Leaving only shadows and stories untold.

04

The Undone Equation

My name is spoken loud in rooms where echoes lie,
A sound that now signifies a vacant, wasted space;
I search for feeling, for the simple sting of pain,
But find only silence where sorrow once had place.

Equations of the heart, unsolved and cold,
Numbers that betray the stories I was told.
Each variable twists, refuses to align,
Leaving fractured patterns, disjointed by design.

I seek the answer, but the sum is gone,
Fractions of a life, scattered at dawn.
The mind races, yet the logic fails,
My voice drowns in an ocean of silent wails.

Still I attempt, though each attempt is vain,
To reconcile the heart with endless pain.
The equation remains, unsolved and undone,
A proof of all that cannot be won.

05

The Geometry of Perpetual End

The clock insists upon a moment turning to the next,
Yet every second is the same unbearable sound;
I am the permanent silence after the earthquake,
A stone praying the tremor will not be found.

Time folds upon itself, looping without release,
Each heartbeat trapped, each breath denied peace.
Shadows lengthen, stretching across empty halls,
Whispering secrets behind the closed walls.

The geometry of sorrow draws circles around my soul,
Infinite angles that no hand can ever control.
Points connect in patterns of despair and night,
Creating structures that block the faintest light.

And yet I stand, a silent witness to the end,
Hoping, perhaps foolishly, that the lines will bend.
Every tick a reminder of what cannot be undone,
Every chime a sorrow as endless as the sun.

23

The Fidelity of Grief

When you lose someone you love,
Your life becomes strange,
The ground beneath you becomes fragile,
Your thoughts make your eyes unsure.

Some dead echo drags your voice down
Where words have no confidence.
Your heart has grown heavy with loss;
And though this loss has wounded others too,
No one knows what has been taken from you
When the silence of absence deepens.

Flickers of guilt kindle regret
For all that was left unsaid or undone.

There are days when you wake up happy,
Again inside the fullness of life,
Until the moment breaks
And you are thrown back
Onto the black tide of loss.

Days when you have your heart back,
You are able to function well,
Until in the middle of work or encounter,
Suddenly with no warning,
You are ambushed by grief.

It becomes hard to trust yourself.
All you can depend on now is that
Sorrow will remain faithful to itself.
More than you, it knows its way
And will find the right time
To pull and pull the rope of grief
Until that coiled hill of tears
Has reduced to its last drop.

Gradually, you will learn acquaintance
With the invisible form of your departed;
And when the work of grief is done,
The wound of loss will heal,
And you will have learned
To wean your eyes
From that gap in the air
And enter the hearth
In your soul where your loved one
Has awaited your return
All the time.

The Rhythm of Loss

Look, the trees are turning their own bodies into pillars of light,
Giving off the rich fragrance of cinnamon and fulfillment bright.

The long tapers of cattails float away over blue shoulders of the ponds,
Each pond, no matter its name, is nameless now, responding to nature’s bonds.

Every year, every lesson I have learned
Leads back to this: the fires and the black river of loss, unearned.

Its other side whispers salvation, hidden and unknown,
A meaning that none of us will ever fully own.

To live, you must love what is mortal, hold it against your bones,
Knowing your own life depends on it, through sighs and moans.

And when the time comes to let it go, let it go with grace,
Release the ghost of presence, leave behind no trace.

Yet still the river flows, the fire burns,
Teaching the heart what each soul learns.
Loss is the teacher, life is the test,
We carry both inside our chest.

To hold, to love, to finally release,
This is the rhythm, the quiet, the peace.

34

A Conversation with Grief

Grief, you did not arrive loudly,
You slipped in like a familiar stranger,
Sat beside me without permission,
And never explained why you stayed.

I tried to keep you outside my hours,
Assigned you moments, corners of the day;
But you spread yourself across my breathing,
Claimed the silence between my thoughts.

You are not cruel, only honest,
You refuse the language of comfort;
You teach me by remaining,
By not leaving when I beg you to.

I learn now to make room for you,
Not as an enemy, not as a guest,
But as a witness to what I loved,
And could not keep alive.

If you must stay, Grief,
Stay gently—
And remind me why the loss mattered.
